The most subscribed channel on YouTube is a position that only one channel on the website can hold at a time. Over time, nine channels have been documented reaching the position. As of March 17, 2014, the most subscribed channel is PewDiePie, with 25 million subscribers. YouTube Spotlight is currently the 2nd most subscribed Channel on YouTube, with 22 million subscribers. The 3rd most subscribed channel is Smosh, with 17 million subscribers.

nigahiga is documented to have the longest consecutive run as the most subscribed channel, with 677 days (701 in total). Smosh holds the record for the highest accumulative amount of days as #1, with 758. Smosh has reached the #1 position on three separate occasions, which is also a record. The total number of days since May 17, 2006 (When the YouTube Subscriber Charts were first released) are 2866. Smosh has been number 1 for slightly over 27% with 759 days.

The ability to "subscribe" to a user's videos was added to YouTube by late October 2005[1] so the numbers of subscribers needed to be most subscribed were relatively small at first. The "most subscribed" list on YouTube began being listed by a chart on the site by May 2006, at which time Smosh was #1 with fewer than 3,000 subscribers. Smosh at that time was more than 1,500 subscribers more than second placed FilthyWhore at 1469.

Note: Though the earliest archived all time list is from May 17 2006, the earliest archived past month list was from May 3, two weeks earlier.

Reactions[edit]

In late 2006 when Peter Oakley aka Geriatric1927 became most subscribed, a number of TV channels wanted to interview him on his rise to fame. The Daily Mail and TechBlog did an article about him and his success.

Following the second time that the channel Smosh became most subscribed, Ray William Johnson collaborated with the duo.[24] A flurry of top YouTubers including Ryan Higa, Shane Dawson, Felix Kjellberg, Michael Buckley, Kassem Gharaibeh, The Fine Brothers, and Johnson himself, congratulated the duo shortly after they surpassed Johnson as the most subscribed channel.[25]

Following Felix Kjellberg's positioning at the top of YouTube, Variety heavily criticized the Swede's videos.[26]
