Sabotage (previously known as Ten and Breacher)[3] is a 2014 American action thriller/crime drama film, directed by David Ayer, written by Skip Woods and Ayer and starring Arnold Schwarzenegger.[4]

Plot[edit]

Members of an elite DEA task force find themselves being taken down one by one after they bust into a drug cartel safe house.

Cast[edit]

  • Arnold Schwarzenegger as John “Breacher” Wharton, the commander of an elite squad of DEA operatives.[5]
  • Sam Worthington as James "Monster" Murray
  • Olivia Williams as Investigator Caroline Brentwood
  • Mireille Enos as Lizzy Murray
  • Terrence Howard as Julius "Sugar" Edmonds
  • Joe Manganiello as Joe "Grinder" Phillips
  • Josh Holloway as Eddie "Neck" Jordan
  • Harold Perrineau as Jackson
  • Martin Donovan as Floyd Morgan
  • Max Martini as Tom "Pyro" Roberts
  • Gary Grubbs as Lou Cantrell
  • Ralf Moeller[6][7]

Production[edit]

Filming started on September 13, 2012 and was completed by January 2013. While the initially released poster for the film gave a release date of April 11, it is scheduled release March 28, 2014. An unrated red band trailer for the film was released on February 7, 2014.[8]

Marketing[edit]

To promote the film, Schwarzenegger and Joe Manganiello guested on the March 24, 2014 episode of WWE Raw, where they joined Hulk Hogan in the ring before confronting The Miz.[9][10]

Reception[edit]

The film has received negative reviews from critics. The film-critic aggregator Rotten Tomatoes lists 22% positive reviews based on 72 reviews, with an average score of 4.2/10. The site's consensus states: "Sabotage boasts one of Arnold Schwarzenegger's finer post-political performances, but it's wasted in a movie driven by grueling violence that punishes seemingly without purpose."[11]
