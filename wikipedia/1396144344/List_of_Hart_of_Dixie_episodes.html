Hart of Dixie is an American television comedy-drama created by Leila Gerstein for The CW, with executive producers, Gerstein, Josh Schwartz, Stephanie Savage, Jason Ensler, and Len Goldstein.[1] The series first airs Monday nights at 9:00 p.m. as a lead-out to Gossip Girl.[1] The series stars Rachel Bilson as Dr. Zoe Hart, who aspires to be like her father and become a cardio-thoracic surgeon. After five years of residency at New York Hospital, Zoe does not get a fellowship and takes up an offer to move south to work in a small medical practice. On April 26, 2013, The CW renewed Hart of Dixie for a third season.[2]

During the first season of the series '&' was formatted into the episode titles with a play on words or alliteration. The only episodes not to feature this were "Pilot", "Hell's Belles", "Heart to Hart", and "The Big Day". The rest of the series uses titles from country music songs as titles of episodes.

As of March 31, 2014[update], 58 episodes of Hart of Dixie have aired.
