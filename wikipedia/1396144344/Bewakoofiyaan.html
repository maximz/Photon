Bewakoofiyaan is a 2014 Indian romantic comedy film directed by Nupur Asthana, written by Habib Faisal and produced by Aditya Chopra under the banner of Yash Raj Films. The film features Ayushmann Khurrana, and Sonam Kapoor in lead roles. The theatrical trailer was launched on February 6, 2014 which was coldly received by Critics and was unable to create the desired buzz for the movie.[3] The music of the film was released on 19 February 2014; it was also received with mixed response from critics.[4] The film released on March 14, 2014[1] coinciding with the commencement of the 2014 ICC World Twenty20. It went on to collect 67.5 million (US$1.1 million) over the first three days of its release.

Production[edit]

Development[edit]

The film was announced in December 2012.[5] The film is written by Habib Faisal (of Ishaqzaade fame) and directed by Nupur Asthana (of Mujhse Fraaandship Karoge fame). The film was described as a "slice of life" film, set during the time of recession. Ayushmann Khurrana's and Sonam Kapoor's characters are described as being "passionately in love" and believe that their love can help them survive, however Kapoor's father, played by Rishi Kapoor, strongly believes against this.[6]

Casting[edit]

Ayushmann Khurrana, Sonam Kapoor and Rishi Kapoor were cast in December 2012. Ayushmann and Sonam are paired opposite each other for the first time, with Rishi Kapoor cast in the role of Sonam Kapoor's father.[5]

Filming[edit]

Shooting for the film began in February 2013[7] in Delhi and Gurgaon.[8][9] Part of the filming took place in Dubai.[10][11]

Critical reception[edit]

On release, the film received mixed responses from critics.[13][14][15][16][17][18][19][20][21]

Taran Adarsh of Bollywood Hungama praised the film, stating in his review the film to be a "pleasant surprise" with "super performances of [sic] Rishi, Ayushmann and Sonam, watertight writing, skilled direction and dollops of humor." Ultimately, he gave the film 4.5 out of 5 stars.[13] Saibal Chatterjee in his review for NDTV described the film to score "with its disarming simplicity" thereby "never less than watchable".[14] Tushar Joshi reviewing for Daily News and Analysis stated it to miss out on the "opportunity to be smarter and funnier" by playing "too safe to rise above the ordinary".[15] Madhureeta Mukherjee in her review for The Times of India stated the film to have its share of beauty but falters with a helpless script.[16]

According to Sanjuta Sharma of Mint and The Wall Street Journal—the film, "flat, monotonous" and "defying all manner of reason" is no better than "a soap opera" throughout the entire course of the film. She stated Khurrana's act to be a "sum of flat expressions and uninspired lines"; that of Rishir Kapoor's to have "hammy moments" and the lack of character evolution or transformation.[17] In three reviews from India Today, Suhani Singh stated it to offer a "a jaded romance and few laughs";[18] Faheem Ruhani asked the readers not to spend their "money on this one";[19] and Rohit Khilnani stated to "skip it".[20] In a review for Hindustan Times by Anupama Chopra stated—"This is one of those films that doesn't either offend or engage. It just goes on, in a bland, listless manner, until we hit happily ever after." She praised Khuranna's performance, saying, "The strongest performance here is Ayushmann’s. His anger and frustration at losing the good life are palpable." Concerning Sonam Kapoor's performance, she said, "Sonam looks lovely. She attempts to imbue Mayera with some texture but it’s an uphill climb."[22] Shubhra Gupta in her review for The Indian Express stated "Neither Kapoor nor Khurrana manage to lift off the screen: they are as flat as the film".[21]

Box office[edit]

The film made on a budget of 220 million (US$3.6 million) including promotional expense of 80 million (US$1.3 million) released on 14 March 2014.[23][24]

Domestic[edit]

The film opened to poor response on the first day of its release.[25] It went on to collect 20 million (US$330,000)—22.5 million (US$370,000) on its first day.[26] It managed to collect 67.5 million (US$1.1 million) over the first three days of its release showing very little growth on it second day, which was necessary for a multiplex-oriented film like itself.[27] It collected another 60 million (US$980,000) to end up with a collection of 127.5 million (US$2.1 million) over the first week of its release.[28]

Overseas[edit]

A report by Rentrak states the film collected in the first three days of its release in – 46,888 United States dollar (INR 28.64 lakhs) releasing in 51 theatres in United States; 20,850 United States dollar (INR 12.74 lakhs) releasing in 15 theatres in Canada; 33,620 pounds (INR 34.12 lakhs) in 26 theatres in United Kingdom and Ireland; 22,145 Australian dollar (INR 12.28 lakhs) in Australia with a screen count of 14; and 12,558 New Zealand dollar (INR 6.56 lakhs) in New Zealand where it has released in 1 theatre.[29]
