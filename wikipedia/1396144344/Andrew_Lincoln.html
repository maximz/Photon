Andrew Lincoln (born Andrew James Clutterbuck; 14 September 1973)[1] is an English actor. He first became known for playing the role of Egg in the BBC drama This Life, followed by other roles such as Simon in the Channel 4 sitcom Teachers and Mark in the British romantic comedy Love Actually. He is now best-known for his portrayal of the character Rick Grimes in the AMC television series The Walking Dead.

Early life[edit]

Lincoln was born in London,[1] his father is an English civil engineer and his mother is a South African nurse of English descent.[2] He grew up in Hull and moved to Bath at the age of 10. He attended Beechen Cliff School where, at the age of fourteen, he had his first acting role playing the Artful Dodger in a school production of Oliver Twist.[3] He spent a summer at the National Youth Theatre in London and became interested in acting as a career.[4] After leaving school, he studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts (RADA), and began to use the stage name Andrew Lincoln.[5]

Career[edit]

Lincoln made his first on-screen appearance in "Births and Deaths", a 1994 episode of Channel 4 sitcom Drop the Dead Donkey.[6] He came to prominence in his first major role playing Edgar "Egg" Cook, one of the lead characters in the acclaimed BBC drama This Life.[7] He went on to star as probationary teacher Simon Casey in the successful Channel 4 sitcom Teachers, and had a lead role as university lecturer and psychologist Robert Bridge in Afterlife. Lincoln appeared in several films, including Human Traffic, and Love Actually which gained him wider recognition. He worked for some time as a voice-over artist,[8] and has also directed two episodes in the third series of Teachers.

In 2009, he appeared in the stage production of Parlour Song.[3] He starred in the Sky One series Strike Back with Richard Armitage,[9] and starred alongside Vanessa Paradis in Pascal Chaumeil's Heartbreaker. In April 2010, Lincoln was cast as Rick Grimes,[10] affecting a Southern USA accent in the live-action adaptation of the comic The Walking Dead,[11] and narrated the project at the 2010 San Diego Comic-Con International.[12] He is signed up for the show for a potential six years.[13]

Personal life[edit]

On 10 June 2006, he married Gael Anderson, daughter of famed Jethro Tull vocalist and flautist, Ian Anderson. At the wedding, Apple Martin (daughter of Gwyneth Paltrow and Chris Martin) was the flower girl.[3] He has two children, Matilda and Arthur.[14] His older brother, Richard Clutterbuck, heads the Bristol Free School.[15]
