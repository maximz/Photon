Kristen Mary "Kris" Jenner (née Houghton, formerly Kardashian; born November 5, 1955)[1][2] is an American television personality, business woman and author who has appeared on Keeping Up with the Kardashians, Kourtney and Khloé Take Miami, Khloé & Lamar, Kourtney and Kim Take New York, Good Morning America, The Talk, America's Next Top Model and Kris.

Divorced from lawyer Robert Kardashian, she has been married to Bruce Jenner since 1991. She has four children with Robert (Kourtney, Kim, Khloé, and Robert) and two with Bruce (Kendall and Kylie Jenner). Jenner's autobiography, Kris Jenner... and All Things Kardashian, was released on November 1, 2011.[3]

Early life[edit]

Jenner was born Kristen Mary Houghton in San Diego, California. She is the daughter of Mary Jo Shannon (née Campbell) and Robert Houghton, an engineer.[4][5] She has English, Scottish, and Dutch ancestry. When Kris was seven years old, her parents divorced and she and her younger sister Karen were raised solely by their mother, until a few years later, when Kris's mother was remarried, this time to businessman Harry Shannon. Three months after moving to Oxnard, California, Harry's business partner ran off with all the company's money, so the family moved back to San Diego.[6] Jenner attended Clairemont High School.[7] Jenner worked as an airline stewardess for a year in 1976.[8]

Marriages and children[edit]

Kris married her first husband, lawyer Robert Kardashian (who later became widely known for his early representation of O.J. Simpson) on July 8, 1978.[9][10] They had four children: daughters Kourtney (born 1979), Kim (born 1980), Khloé (born 1984) and son Rob (born 1987). The couple divorced in March 1991, just one month before Kris married her second husband, Bruce Jenner. Kris and Robert continued to be close friends after the divorce, up through Robert's diagnosis with esophageal cancer and his death in 2003.[11] In 2012, Kris confessed she had an affair with former soccer player and animator Todd Waterman during her marriage to Robert. She referred to Waterman as "Ryan" in her autobiography, but he revealed his identity on his own.[12] They had an encounter on Keeping up with the Kardashians while Kris was having a tennis lesson. Todd is then revealed on the show.[clarification needed][citation needed]

She married her second husband, former Olympian Bruce Jenner, in April 1991.[13] She and Bruce have two daughters together: Kendall (born 1995) and Kylie (born 1997).[14] The two announced their separation in October 2013.[15][16] Through Bruce, Kris has four stepchildren: Burton, Brandon, Brody and Cassandra Lynn "Casey" Jenner.

Kris Jenner runs her own production company Jenner Communications which is based in Los Angeles. Since the start of Keeping Up With The Kardashians, she has managed her daughter Kim's career. She also is involved with the management of her other daughters and son. She has three grandchildren.[17][18][19]

Involvement in O.J. Simpson trial[edit]

The Kardashian family was very close to O.J. Simpson and Nicole Brown Simpson. The Simpson trial caused conflict between Kris and her former husband. Robert Kardashian was part of O.J. Simpson's defense team, as Nicole Simpson had been Kris's best friend prior to her death.[20]

In her book, Kris explained that she named her daughter Kendall Nicole after Nicole Brown Simpson. Kris wore maternity clothing during the trial that she had gotten from Nicole before she died. (Nicole Brown Simpson was murdered in June 1994, and Kendall Jenner was born in November 1995 meaning that Nicole must have given the clothes to Kris several months before Kris got pregnant.) [21]

Plastic surgery[edit]

As recounted in her memoir, Kris Jenner and All Things Kardashian, Jenner underwent plastic surgery to enhance her breasts twice and has had extensive work done on her nose as well as many counts of botox. In June 2011, Jenner received plastic surgery months before her daughter Kim's wedding. She allowed the preparations and recovery from the surgery to be recorded. The video footage can be seen on E! Online and "Kim's Fairytale Wedding Part 1" episode of Keeping Up With the Kardashians.[22] She explained: "I needed a little freshening up for the wedding. Just doing what a girl's gotta do!"[23]

Kris[edit]

Jenner hosted a pop culture-driven daytime talk show, Kris. The series began its six-week trial summer run on several Fox-owned stations on July 15, 2013.[24] Kanye West revealed the first public picture of daughter North West on Kris Jenner's daytime talk show. The show did not have its six-week trial run extended by Fox.[25]
