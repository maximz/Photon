Resurrection is an American fantasy drama television series about dead people who return to life. The series is based on the book The Returned by Jason Mott. Although similar in concept to Les Revenants, it is based on a different source, and Resurrection has no connection to the French movie or television series.[1][2] The series is being co-produced by ABC Studios and Plan B Entertainment, led by Brad Pitt, Aaron Zelman, JoAnn Alfano, Jon Liebman, Dede Gardner, Jeremy Kleiner, Michele Fazekas, and Tara Butters. The pilot episode, named after the book on which the series is based, was ordered to series on May 10, 2013[3] and was placed on the schedule as a limited run during the 2013–14 television season.[4] The series premiered on March 9, 2014.[5]

Premise[edit]

The series follows the residents of Arcadia, Missouri, whose lives are upended when their loved ones return from the dead, unaged since their deaths. Among the returned is Jacob Langston, an eight-year-old boy who drowned 32 years earlier. Having somehow been found alive in China, he is brought back to America by an immigration agent. His surprise return inspires the local sheriff, whose wife presumably drowned trying to rescue Jacob, to learn more about this mystery.

Development and production[edit]

In January 2013, ABC ordered the project to pilot status[6] and in March brought in Charles McDougall as director as part of a two-year deal he signed with ABC Studios.[7] On March 7, 2013, ABC released its list of potential hour-long pilot pick-ups, with the title changed to Forever.[8] The title changed again after ABC ordered it to series in May 2013.[9]

Critical reception[edit]

Resurrection scored 59 out of 100 on Metacritic based on 28 "mixed or average" reviews.[10] On another review aggregator site, Rotten Tomatoes, it holds a 53% rating with an average rating of 5.7 out of 10, based on 32 reviews.[11]

Broadcast[edit]

In Canada, the series is simsubbed on City.

In Spain, the series airs on Sony Pictures Entertainment's AXN from March 20, 2014, after 10 pm.[12]

In Australia, the series airs on the Seven Network in Australia from March 25, 2014, at 8:45 pm,[13] and will air on TVNZ's TV2 in New Zealand at a later date.
