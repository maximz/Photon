Race Gurram (English: Race horse) is an upcoming Telugu family drama film directed by Surender Reddy starring Allu Arjun, Shruti Haasan, Saloni Aswani and Shaam. Nallamalapu Srinivas (Bujji) and Dr. Venkateswara Rao are producing this film under Lakshmi Narasimha production and S. Thaman provided the music.[2] The cinematography is handled by Manoj Paramahamsa. Vakkantham Vamsi penned the script while Goutham Raju handled the editing department. The film is also the first Telugu movie to feature Auro 11.1 Audio file format, the next-generation three-layer sound technology from Auro Technologies, powered by Barco Sounds.[3]

The Principal photography started on May 13, 2013 in Hyderabad.[4] The film's talkie part completed on December 24, 2013.[5] The film's entire shoot completed on February 22, 2014.[6] The film is slated for a worldwide release on April 11, 2014 across the world.[1] Malayalam Version Of Race Gurram Title Has Been Registered As Lucky The Racer And It Will Be Released Simultaneously With The Original Version.

Production[edit]

Development[edit]

The film was launched on 24 October 2012, the day of Vijayadasami, at Ramanaidu Studios in Hyderabad. Chiranjeevi, Allu Aravind, Nallamalupu Srinivas (Bujji), Dr. Venkateswara Rao, D. Ramanaidu, K. Raghavendra Rao, Nagababu, Ali, V. V. Vinayak, Rana Daggubati, Shyam Prasad Reddy, Vakkantham Vamsi graced the event.[7] D. Ramanaidu did the formal pooja and Chiranjeevi gave the clap. V. V. Vinayak directed the first shot while Shyam Prasad Reddy switched on the Camera. One of the producers, Dr. Venkateshwar Rao said that the Regular shoot would start in December after the Completion of Allu Arjun's Iddarammayilatho.[8] However, It was declared that the regular shooting would start from May 2, 2013 aiming a Vijaya Dasami Release. Later is said that the movie is set to release in the Mid of Feb 2014.[9]

Casting[edit]

Surender Reddy is directing this film which is produced by Nallamalupu Bujji. This would be the first film for Allu Arjun with Surender Reddy and S. Thaman. Initially Samantha was approached for female lead in it, but later Shruti Haasan was selected as the Heroine, which was confirmed by the actress in her twitter.[10] This film is said to be a complete youthful action romantic entertainer.[11] Vijay would provide the fights and thrills in the film.[8] Surender Reddy roped in Shaam after he worked with him in Kick and Oosaravelli.[12] Saloni Aswani is going to be seen in the film as the second heroine.[13] Bhojpuri Superstar Ravi Kishan was roped in as the antagonist thus marking this film his Telugu Debut.[14] In February 2014, it was reported that Veteran Kannada and Telugu actress Pavitra Lokesh is playing the role of Allu Arjun's Mother in this film.[15] In Mid March 2014, it was reported that Brahmanandam would play the role of a police officer in this film.[16]

Filming[edit]

However, due to some delay, the regular shooting started from May 13, 2013 in Hyderabad.[17] After completing the first schedule in Hyderabad, the team headed to Norway for canning some important scenes including some of the songs, making it a long schedule.[18] After the completion of the shoot, the unit proceeded to Europe where 2 songs were scheduled to be canned in a period of 8 days ranging from July 1, 2013 after which the regular shoot would continue in Hyderabd.[19] However, the shooting resumed in Geneva which later continued in Milan.[20] Later on the unit returned to Hyderabad and Allu Arjun and Shruti Haasan participated in the shooting at Annapurna Studios 7 Acres.[21] There the song Ek Do Teen Char Paanch Pataka Neeku Naaku was shot under the choreography of Johnny Master.[22]

After the completion of the song shoot, Allu Arjun participated in a fight scene shoot which was choreographed by noted fight masters Ram-Lakshman.[23] Later in September, some scenes were canned on Allu Arjun and others at Golconda Fort in Hyderabad.[24] Later on some fight scenes were canned in and around Hyderabad in which the leads participated.[25] On the night of October 28, 2013 scenes between Allu Arjun and Shaam were shot at Hell pad Junction in Ramoji Film City at Hyderabad.[26] In Early November 2013, shooting continued in Ramoji Film City where some comedy scenes were shot.[27] From November 16, 2013 some action sequences were canned in which Allu Arjun participated.[28] The shooting continued in Ramoji Film City and the unit shot some more fight sequences at the outskirts of Hyderabad from November 19, 2013.[29]

The shooting continued in Jadcherla where scenes on Allu Arjun, Brahmanandam and Ravi Kishan were shot.[30] Later some crucial scenes were shot near Charminar area in Hyderabad.[31] After wrapping up a chase sequence on Allu Arjun and Shruti Haasan there, the unit yet again continued its shooting at Ramoji Film City. By then Surender Reddy revealed that most of the film's shoot ha been wrapped up.[32] On December 24, it was announced that the film's talkie part was completed and 2 songs were left to be shot which would start form the new schedule which would commence from January 5, 2014.[33] On January 5, the new schedule yet again began at Ramoji Film City, Hyderabad where some crucial scenes and an item number will be shot. It was said that the last song's shoot was said to be held from January 20 to 25 with which the Principal photography would come to an end.[34]

Later on January 13, 2014 a song was shot on Allu Arjun and Kaira Dutta in which Thagubothu Ramesh and Srinivas Reddy also participated.[35] It was said to be the introduction song of Allu Arjun in the film which was composed by Dinesh Master.[36] A song was planned to be shot on Allu Arjun and Shruti Haasan from February 15.[37] However, since the latter had a surgery, the filming of the song was shifted to February 17.[38] On February 20 Shruti Haasan announced that she wrapped up her part of the shoot.[39] The song's shoot came to an end on February 22, 2014 thus ending the film's shoot.[6]Race Gurram is said to be made on a whooping budget of 55 crore (US$9.0 million) due to scirpt requirments.

Soundtrack[edit]

Main article: Race Gurram (soundtrack)

S. Thaman composed the Music and Background Score for this film which marks his first collaboration with Allu Arjun and second collaboration with Surender Reddy.[40] In January 2014, Lahari Music bagged the Audio rights for an amount of 5.4 million.[41] Later in early March 2014, Thaman confirmed that the film's audio would be launched on March 16 at Hyderabad.[42] The film's Soundtrack Album was released in a simple and formal promotional event on March 16, 2014 at Park Hyatt hotel in Hyderabad.[43] The Audio received predominant positive response from all corners and many celebrities praised Thaman for his work.[44] The soundtrack also debuted as one among the top ten albums in iTunes along with Maan Karate and Kochadaiiyaan.[45]

Marketing[edit]

The film's first look released on December 7, 2013.[46] The teaser was released on the same day at 10:00 AM in the Morning on the occasion of Surender Reddy's birthday in YouTube.[47] The Teaser was of 30 seconds in which Arjun runs in a Black shirt and Black Trousers with Black Goggles with the Background Music notching the word Buchodu (Bogeyman) with Ravi Kishan's eyes highlighted twice. The teaser received positive response from fans of Arjun as well as common audience.[48] The Audio release poster featuring Allu Arjun and Shruti Haasan was released on March 13, 2014 in High Quality version after the low quality version released into the Internet.[49] That poster received viral response from all corners.[50] On March 16, 2014 the teasers of all the six songs were released into YouTube officially by the makers.[51]

Before the music launch was held in a low key affair at Park Hyatt, it was reported that the makers opted for organizing a big fanfare event before the film's release.[43] After the Audio released into the market, it was reported that the film's theatrical trailer would be launched in a function held at Rajahmundry on March 22, 2014.[52] The venue was confirmed on March 21, 2014 as the GIET college youth fest in Rajamundry.[53] The trailer received positive response from all corners.[54] It was reported that the producers ordered unique light based standees featuring a dancing still of Allu Arjun and each of these standees costed around 10,000. It was said that the producer has ordered around 50 standees and keep it prominently in all multiplexes and important single screens.[55]

Release[edit]

Initially, the film was planned as a Vijayadashami release viz. October 13, 2013.[56] However it was later shifted to January 2014 as a Sankranti special along with Mahesh Babu's 1 - Nenokkadine.[57] In the end of November 2013, it was confirmed that the film would be using the Auro 11.1 Audio file format by Barco Sounds thus making it the third Telugu movie to adopt this next-generation three-layer sound technology from Auro Technologies, powered by Barco Sounds which was succeeded by Mahesh Babu's 1 - Nenokkadine.[58] But in early December 2013, it was confirmed that the film's release postponed again thus exiting the Sankranti race.[59] Then it was declared that it would hit the screens in February 2014.[60] In the end of December 2013, Reports emerged that the film postponed yet again from February and was slated as a Summer Release.[61]

In the end of January 2014, it was reported that the film would release worldwide on April 4, 2014.[62] In Mid February 2014, it was reported that the film is expected to release on April 11, 2014.[6] But on the last day of February 2014, it was reported that the film would release on March 28, 2014 clashing with Nandamuri Balakrishna's Legend which was also slated for a March 28, 2014 release worldwide.[63] On March 13, 2014 FICUS Inc released a press note which stated that they bagged the entire overseas theatrical screening of the film.[64] During the theatrical trailer launch at Rajahmundry, Allu Arjun confirmed that the film would release on April 11, 2014.[1]
