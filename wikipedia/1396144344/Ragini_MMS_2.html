Ragini MMS 2 is a Bollywood Horror-Thriller film directed by Bhushan Patel and co-produced by Ekta Kapoor and Shobha Kapoor under Balaji Motion Pictures and ALT Entertainment.[3][4] The movie is the sequel to the 2011 horror movie Ragini MMS. However with different casts, Ragini MMS 2 features Sunny Leone and the newcomer Saahil Prem on lead roles continuing the story from the prequel.

Plot[edit]

The movie begins from where the prequel ended. Ragini's MMS goes viral and this catches the attention of a failed director, Rocks (Parvin Dabas) who decides to make a movie on the scandal. He signs Sunny (Sunny Leone) as the lead actress and shoots the movie in the same house.

Sunny, who takes an instant liking to the script requests the director to let her meet the real Ragini (Kainaz Motivala) who is now in asylum, to get a better in depth look of the character she wants to portray. But the meeting goes horribly wrong when Ragini stabs herself brutally in her neck, killing herself.

Later, the cast and crew arrive at the haunted house to shoot and things start going haywire. Then entered Dr Meera Dutta (Divya Dutta), a psychiatrist who specializes in such horrible cases. After going through old news paper clipping and video tapes of Ragini, She finds the truth regarding the ghost.

It was then revealed that the ghost in other life was a married women with two daughters and a son. She was much attached towards her son whom she conceived after long prayers. One day, the boy and his two sisters were playing hide and seek, then the boy slips into well and dies, driving his mother insane and in quest to resurrect him from the dead. Upon believing the two black magic performing priest, the mother sacrifices both the daughters. When the villagers finally arrived, they assumed that the mother might be practicing witch craft and punished her by burning her alive hanging from a tree. The women finally drops from tree and after cursing all, kills herself by stabbing in her neck with the rattle toy with which her son used to play.

Mrs Dutta then rushes to the shooting location in order to inform the cast and crew to abandon the shooting in that place and to leave that house immediately. But it was too late as the ghost has already possessed Sunny and started killing the junior artists along with supporting cast by either luring them into having sex or killing them by catching them unguarded when they are all alone. Only the scriptwriter Satya (Saahil Prem) and one side actor Monali (Sandhya Mridul) are left alive by running for their lives from the ghost-possessed Sunny.

As the trio fights out Sunny, Monali gets killed. When Satya accidentally steps on the rattle toy with which the mother committed suicide prior to becoming a ghost, it caused pain to Sunny temporarily and thereby subduing the ghost to temporarily leave Sunny. Mrs Dutta comes to know about the situation and urges Satya to destroy it. Eventually the ghost is destroyed along with the rattle. And at last, Sunny, Satya and Mrs Dutta left the house solving the mystery once and for ever as they thought, but at the end when they leave it shows a rocking chair rocking back and forth and a child's voice, signifying that there is still a ghost in the house and maybe a continuing story.

Production[edit]

Sunny Leone, who plays female lead in Ragini MMS 2, says this movie is more commercial in nature than its prequel. Director Bhushan Patel says that there is a lot more sex, scares and a lot of glamour with the songs.[6] The movie was scheduled to be released in the month of October 2013. However, Balaji Motion Pictures officially confirmed the release date, scheduled to be on 17 January 2014. The release date of the film was further postponed to 21 March 2014.[7]

Release[edit]

Critical Reception[edit]

Ragini MMS 2 received mixed responses from movie critics. Taran Adarsh of Bollywood Hungama rated the movie 4½ out of 5 and stated, "RAGINI MMS-2 has ample scares, a generous dose of skin show, a winning soundtrack and of course, a sensuous Sunny Leone to spell S-U-C-C-E-S-S. This second helping is much more scrumptious and delicious than the first. Go, get spooked!"[12]

Avad M of Bollywood3 stated, "Undoubtedly, Sunny Leone is the major attraction of ‘Ragini MMS 2’. Those who go to the theatres expecting to see Sunny’s glamour and sensuous body, will not be disappointed at all."[13]

Madhureeta Mukherjee of the Times of India rated the movie 3 out 5 and said, "The film provides the usual creepy cliches - creaking windows, ghostly shadows and bedraggled ghosts. There are a few spooky moments, but fewer leap-out-of-your-seat scenes. Sunny looks deathly desirable and plays the sexed-up baby doll with abandon. While her 'act' is good, her 'performance' doesn't really climax. Yet, she gives us a 'drool-worthy' adult-horror film - one of its kind for Bollywood."[14]

Mohar Basu of Koimoi rated the movie 3 out 5 concluding, "Ragini MMS 2 is quite a pleasant surprise. It is not everyday that you begin your morning watching a Sunny Leone film, where she actually manages to bewilder you something close to a performance. Interestingly threaded with ample hair raising minutes, the film’s ability to seem unrelentingly real is what makes it an entertaining watch. Not worth an Oscar but worth spending your popcorn over this weekend for sure."[15]

Komal Nahta writes on his blog, "Bhushan Patel’s direction is first-rate. He has made a film which does not lose its grip on the audience even for a minute, keeping them engrossed right from the word ‘go’. He has balanced the drama with good doses of sex, horror and comedy so as to give every kind of audience entertainment to the fullest. On the whole, Ragini MMS – 2 is a surefire hit as it has the deadly combination of sex, horror, comedy and hit music. It will please the masses and classes alike and will work wonders at the box-office from North to South and East to West."[16]

Shubhra Gupta of The Indian Express rated the movie 2.5 out 5 stating, "Despite its harum-scarum script and barely-there logic, I quite enjoyed ‘Ragini MMS 2’ in the portions when Sunny Leone is in full stride.Where it falters is in the lack of novelty in the scenes that feature the spirit, and its bloody trail: there are a couple of scenes that send a shiver down your spine, but then it all becomes familiar. A little more attention to that, and to the storyline, and this will become a solid franchise."[17]

Namita Karkera of Rediff.com gave the film a score of 1.5 out of 5 noting, "Sunny Leone looks hot alright but with scary scenes being average at best, Ragini MMS 2 isn’t half as scary as it is supposed to be. At best, the film seems like a spoof. The second half of the movie however fares better. Overall the movie still manages to scare at some shots, make us laugh and enjoy Sunny Leone in her skimpy outfits."[18]

Box Office[edit]

Domestic[edit]

Ragini MMS 2 had a very strong opening day with the film collecting 8.45 crore despite competition from T20 World cup. Collections dropped on second day with the film collecting 7.35 crore on second day. Collections rose again on third day as the film collected 8.78 crore on the third day leading to an opening weekend of 24.5 crores.[19] The film continued its steady run at the box office as it collected 38.5 crore on its opening week.[20]
