Log Horizon (ログ・ホライズン, Rogu Horaizun?) is a Japanese novel series written by Mamare Touno. It was initially posted on the user-generated content site Shōsetsuka ni Narō in 2010 and subsequently published by Enterbrain since March 2011. The series follows the strategist, Shiroe, and the other players of the long-lived MMORPG Elder Tale after they find themselves whisked away into the game world following a game update. The novel has received four manga adaptations, with one based on the main story and the other three revolving around characters in the series. An anime adaptation aired on NHK Educational TV between October 5, 2013 and March 22, 2014. A second season will begin airing in Fall 2014.

Plot[edit]

Main article: List of Log Horizon characters

By its eleventh expansion pack, the massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) Elder Tale (エルダー・テイル, Erudā Teiru?) has become a global success, with a user base of millions of players. However, during the release of its twelfth expansion pack: Novasphere Pioneers (ノウアスフィアの開墾, Nōasufia no Kaikon?, alt. Homesteading the Noosphere[1][2]), thirty thousand Japanese gamers who are all logged on at the time of the update, suddenly find themselves transported into the virtual game world and donning their in-game avatars. In the midst of the event, a socially awkward gamer named Shiroe, along with his friends, Naotsugu and Akatsuki, decide to team up so that they may face this world, which unfortunately has now become their reality, along with the challenges and obstacles ahead of them.

Manga[edit]

The novel series has received four manga adaptations, all written by Mamare Touno. The first adaptation is illustrated by Motoya Matsu and titled, Log Horizon Gaiden: Honey Moon Logs.[11] It began serialization on January 27, 2012, and is published by ASCII Media Works in the Dengeki Daioh magazine.[11] The second adaptation is illustrated by Kazuhiro Hara and titled Log Horizon.[12] It began serialization on May 18, 2012, and is published by Enterbrain in the Famitsu Comic Clear web magazine.[12] The third adaptation is illustrated by Koyuki and titled, Log Horizon: Nishikaze no Ryōdan.[13] It began serialization on July 9, 2012, and is published by Fujimi Shobo in the Age Premium magazine.[13] The last adaptation is illustrated by Sōchū and titled, Log Horizon Gaiden: Nyanta-honcho Shiawase no Recipe.[14] It began serialization on December 21, 2012, and is published by Enterbrain in the Comic B's LOG magazine.[14]

Anime[edit]

See also: List of Log Horizon episodes (Season 1)

A 25-episode anime adaptation produced by Satelight began airing on NHK Educational TV on October 5, 2013.[22] The series was streamed as a simulcast by Crunchyroll in North America and other select parts of the world.[23] The anime has been licensed by Sentai Filmworks in North America for digital and home video release in 2014.[24] The anime ended on March 22, 2014. The opening theme is "database" by Man with a Mission featuring Takuma while the ending theme is "Your song*" by Yun*chi.[25] A second season will begin airing in Fall 2014.[26]
