Log Horizon („É≠„Ç∞„Éª„Éõ„É©„Ç§„Ç∫„É≥, Rogu Horaizun?) is a Japanese novel series written by Mamare Touno. It was initially posted on the user-generated content site Sh≈çsetsuka ni Nar≈ç in 2010 and subsequently published by Enterbrain since March 2011. The series follows the strategist, Shiroe, and the other players of the long-lived MMORPG Elder Tale after they find themselves whisked away into the game world following a game update. The novel has received four manga adaptations, with one based on the main story and the other three revolving around characters in the series. An anime adaptation aired on NHK Educational TV between October 5, 2013 and March 22, 2014. A second season will begin airing in Fall 2014.

Manga[edit]

The novel series has received four manga adaptations, all written by Mamare Touno. The first adaptation is illustrated by Motoya Matsu and titled, Log Horizon Gaiden: Honey Moon Logs.[11] It began serialization on January 27, 2012, and is published by ASCII Media Works in the Dengeki Daioh magazine.[11] The second adaptation is illustrated by Kazuhiro Hara and titled Log Horizon.[12] It began serialization on May 18, 2012, and is published by Enterbrain in the Famitsu Comic Clear web magazine.[12] The third adaptation is illustrated by Koyuki and titled, Log Horizon: Nishikaze no Ry≈çdan.[13] It began serialization on July 9, 2012, and is published by Fujimi Shobo in the Age Premium magazine.[13] The last adaptation is illustrated by S≈çch≈´ and titled, Log Horizon Gaiden: Nyanta-honcho Shiawase no Recipe.[14] It began serialization on December 21, 2012, and is published by Enterbrain in the Comic B's LOG magazine.[14]

Anime[edit]

See also: List of Log Horizon episodes (Season 1)

A 25-episode anime adaptation produced by Satelight began airing on NHK Educational TV on October 5, 2013.[22] The series was streamed as a simulcast by Crunchyroll in North America and other select parts of the world.[23] The anime has been licensed by Sentai Filmworks in North America for digital and home video release in 2014.[24] The anime ended on March 22, 2014. The opening theme is "database" by Man with a Mission featuring Takuma while the ending theme is "Your song*" by Yun*chi.[25] A second season will begin airing in Fall 2014.[26]
