Queen is a 2014 Bollywood coming-of-age comedy-drama film by Viacom 18 Motion Pictures and Phantom Films. Directed by Vikas Bahl, the film stars Kangana Ranaut, Lisa Haydon and Rajkummar Rao in lead roles.[2] The film’s story is about a middle class Indian girl in Rajouri Garden, Delhi, who decides to go on her honeymoon to Paris and Amsterdam alone, after her fiancé walks out of the marriage two days before wedding.[3] Amit Trivedi has scored the film’s music for the lyrics by Anvita Dutt. The film premiered at Busan International Film Festival in October 2013, and released worldwide in 800 Screens on March 7, 2014.Queen collected 71.12 crore (US$12 million) Gross in 3 weeks declared to be a Blockbuster.[4] Queen collected 50 crore (US$8.2 million) of domestic nett collections.

Plot[edit]

Rani (Kangana Ranaut) is a 21-year-old Punjabi girl staying in Rajouri Garden, Delhi. She is from a very conservative family; her brother escorts her everywhere for her safety. Two days before her marriage, her fiancé Vijay (Rajkummar Rao) meets her in a local cafe and announces his unwillingness to get married to her. He cites that he has changed, and she would not match his lifestyle now as the reason for calling off the wedding. Shocked at the development, Rani shuts herself in her room for a day.

She decides to take control of her life and plans to go alone on her pre-booked honeymoon to visit her favourite place, Paris, and Vijay's favourite place, Amsterdam. After initial hesitation, her parents agree and Rani departs from Delhi.

At Paris she meets Vijayalakshmi aka Vijay (Lisa Haydon), who has a French/Spanish mother and an Indian father. She works in the hotel where Rani stays. Initially reluctant and having gotten into trouble twice - once with the local police and once with a robber - Rani decides to return to India. But soon Vijayalakshmi becomes her friend and confidante, and shows her around Paris. Rani enjoys her company and they fall into a series of humorous situations. On one such occasion, Rani sends her selfie clicked in western outfit and sends it to Vijay, instead of Vijayalakshmi, and soon realises her mistake. Rani relives the memories of Vijay patronising her for so many of the things which she is not allowed to do. Rani dances, drinks, and lets herself free in Paris.

She bids an emotional farewell to Vijayalakshmi and boards a train for Amsterdam. Arrive Amsterdam and hostel, which is on sharing basis. With no option and contact to make, she agrees to move into the room on shared basis with three other guys. Meet Taka, Tim and Oleksander. Soon, the four become good friends and move around the city shopping, sight seeing, visiting a sex shop, and meeting pole dancers in a club. Here Rani's character builds confidence. Rani drives her friends when drunk and earns money in cook-off on sailor front by selling gol gappa. She learns stories about her new friends and understands how different life can be.

One day, after returning from Cook off, Rani encounters Vijay who has been eagerly waiting for her outside her hotel. He apologies and asks her to reconsider the relation. Rani advises him to leave after he tries to drag her in front of her friends and they retaliate.

Next day Rani meets Vijay and says she will speak with him in Delhi upon her return. She meets her friends for one last time in the rock show she previously cancelled. After bidding emotional farewell and realising she has to face her life, Rani returns to Delhi as a changed person.

Upon arrival, she meets Vijay and without saying anything, she hands him her engagement ring and thanks him. Rani leaves his house and is relieved and relaxed.

Development[edit]

Producer-director Vikas Bahl made his directorial debut with Chillar Party (2011), co-directed with Nitish Tiwari, which won three National Film Awards. Since he liked the story of Queen, he decided to direct the film himself. [5] It was eventually produced by Viacom 18 Motion Pictures and Phantom Films, which he co-owns with Anurag Kashyap and Vikram Motwane.[6]

He wrote the script, the character of Rani was based on the people he had observed while growing up in Delhi. "I know life for girls is planned out for them by their families. They lose their own perspective on life and they are okay with that."[7] Bahl wrote the script of the movie keeping in mind Kangana Ranaut, however it was Anurag Basu who connected him with Kangana and helped cast her in the film.[8] Kareena Kapoor was approached for the role of Rani, but she rejected the film.[9]

After her audition, actress Lisa Haydon, who played an Indian-French hotel staff, Vijaylaksmi, practiced French for a month. Other actors Boyko Mish (Olik) and Jeffery Ho (Taka) were casted after auditions in London, while Joseph Guitobh (Tim), who played Rani's third roommate, was spotted singing on a street and was asked whether he'd be interested in working in Bollywood film, though he couldn't speak English. [7]

Filming[edit]

The film was in late 2012 shot over period of 45 days, starting with Paris, followed by Amsterdam and Delhi.[7] Despite shooting abroad, Vikas Bahl did not have a huge budget to spend on the film. He took a crew of 25 people from India to shoot the film in about 145 locations in 40 days. Locations were booked a couple of hours, before the crew would rush to another location as they often shot 3-4 locations in a day. The crew would eat their meals at nearest restaurants, and at times actors including lead Kangana Ranaut would change clothes in near by public toilets and restaurants. [10][5] Since the film was was not shot in a linear fashion, the colour of mehndi, applied to bride's hand was faded to match its natural fading.[7] During the filming upon Ranaut's suggestion some scenes were added, like the kiss scene with the Italian chef, and a small scene where Rani asks a stranger to click her picture in Amsterdam.[7] She even ended up writing a lot of her dialogues.[5]

The remixed version of "Hungama Ho Gaya" was shot at Club NL, in Amsterdam, where a Bollywood number was played for the first time.[11][12]

However, when 90 percent of the film was already shot, film's cinematographer Bobby Singh died suddenly following an asthma attack on December 25, 2012, after just having completed a schedule in Delhi. [13][14] After seeing rushes of the film, Anurag Kashyap volunteered to edit the film himself and since Bahl didn't have any editors at the time, he too agreed. [5]

Marketing[edit]

The trailer of the film released on 20 December 2013 and was well received by critics.[15]

Soundtrack[edit]

Main article: Queen (soundtrack)

The film's soundtrack is composed by Amit Trivedi while lyrics are by Anvita Dutt.[16] The Music of Queen was launched at Kala Ghoda Arts Festival, Mumbai on February 2, 2014.[17] An additional track was released on March 1, it was the remixed version of hit cabaret number of the 1970s Hungama Ho Gaya sung by Asha Bhosle for the film, Anhonee (1973) was remixed by Amit Trivedi for the film, with additional vocals by Arijit Singh. The song was also used in end credits of the film.[18][11][12]

Reception[edit]

Critical Reception[edit]

Queen met with critical acclaim. Kangana Ranaut's performance was especially praised by critics.[19]

Saibal Chatterjee of NDTV gave the film 4/5 stars and called the film "spicy, balmy and uplifting" and added, "Kangana’s is the heart and soul of Queen and she does not strike a single false note".[20] Raja Sen from Rediff also awarded the film 4/5 stars and called Kangana 'gobstoppingly spectacular' and said the film is 'a showcase for an actress poised to reign'.[21] Rajeev Masand of CNN-IBN gave the film 4/5 stars calling the film "rare and disarming" and described Kangana's performance as " a raw, nuanced, delicately comical"[22] Anupama Chopra praised Kangana's performance, hailing it as "artfully transparent and heartfelt", but said the film is "meandering and indulgent" at places and gave the film 3.5/5 stars.[23] Subhash K. Jha gave the film 4.5/5 stars and stated, "Queen is the quintessential inspirational tale with a central performance that makes most of what we see in Hindi cinema look like put-on slide shows."[24] Sarita Tanwar from Daily News and Analysis awarded the film 4.5/5 stars and said, "Queen is irresistible. Highly recommended. A must-see film. You will leave the theatre with your heart humming happily."[25] Saurabh Dwivedi from India Today also gave the film 4.5/5 stars and said the film is "amazing" and "a brilliant package".[26] Deepanjana Pal from Firstpost has quoted "Kangana Ranaut is pitch perfect in Queen"[27]Queen was made on a budget of 12 crore (US$2.0 million) turned out to be smashing Hit by collecting 71 crore (US$12 million) in 3 weeks.[28]

Box office[edit]

Released on 7 March 2014, this film opened on 800 screens in comparison to 850 for Total Siyapaa and 1,600 screens for Gulaab Gang, releasing on the same date, facing tough competition.[29][30] Queen debuted with a relatively low collections and generated 2 crore (US$330,000) on the first day, but grew on Saturday as it collected 3.35 crore (US$550,000). and showed another jump on Sunday, collecting 4.3 crore (US$700,000)., taking the first weekend's collection to 9.5 crore (US$1.5 million).[31] Despite poor start, Queen held up well in the first weekdays and collected around 2.5 crore (US$410,000). per day during week day and ended first week with 18 crore (US$2.9 million).[32] Movie collected 9.5 crore (US$1.5 million) in its second weekend which was fantastic comparing other releases had so far in 2014. On its second Monday movie collected 3 crore (US$490,000) due its been a Holiday in India.[33] In a rare occurrence on Indian box office, Queen had higher collection in the second week than the first week, collecting 39 crore (US$6.4 million) in two weeks.[34][35] For the first time since Gadar: Ek Prem Katha in 2001, a film had higher collection in the second week than the first.[36] The film collected 50.5 crore (US$8.2 million) in three weeks, emerging as a box office hit.[1]

In International market movie collected below par in first weekend and was on second place after Total Siyapaa.[37]

Queen collected 71 crore (US$12 million) gross in 3 weeks delivering 50 crore (US$8.2 million) Nett in 3 weeks worldwide.

Queen approximately collected a gross of 71.12 crore (US$12 million) with a Nett outcome of 49.2 crore (US$8.0 million) Share in Just 19 Days.[38]
