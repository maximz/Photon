Myles Jury[2] (born October 31, 1988) is an American mixed martial artist. He is currently signed with the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC), fighting in their lightweight division. As of March 17, 2014, he is #13 in official UFC lightweight rankings.[3]

Mixed martial arts career[edit]

Early career[edit]

Jury made his professional MMA debut in October 2005. Prior to appearing on The Ultimate Fighter, he competed primarily for the King of the Cage promotion in California. In his first 5 years as a fighter he amassed a record of nine wins and no losses, with all of his wins coming in the first round. He earned his Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu Black Belt under Don Richard (who is a black belt under Master Caique).[1] From there he started his own MMA Affiliate system, Jury Jiu Jitsu, teaching Wrestling, Striking, and Jiu Jitsu. His goal is simply teach his new era style for those wanting to learn, work out or become an elite fighter. Jury said most importantly, "his system also can take a person like himself that had nothing going for him, to a better way of life and the outlet anyone needs."

Jury was raised in a broken home and credits his coach, Don Richard, for taking him under his wing at 12 years old and making him who he is today, with the positive outlook, dedication, and hard worker.

The Ultimate Fighter[edit]

Myles Jury had two stints on the Ultimate Fighter. His first stint was on TUF 13, when he tore his ACL in the first episode and was sidelined for a year without being able to train. But he was able to compete on TUF 15 as was one of the 32 lightweight fighters announced by the UFC to participate in first live season of The Ultimate Fighter reality show.[4]

Jury won his fight to get in the house, then was selected to fight Al Iaquinta and was defeated by via split decision after three rounds, which won fight of the season.[5]

Ultimate Fighting Championship[edit]

Jury officially made his UFC debut at The Ultimate Fighter 15 Finale on June 1, 2012 against Chris Saunders.[6] He won the fight via submission in the first round.

Jury next fought Michael Johnson on December 29, 2012 at UFC 155.[7] Jury dominated Johnson for all three rounds to earn a unanimous decision.

In his third UFC appearance, Jury faced Ramsey Nijem on April 20, 2013 at UFC on Fox 7.[8] He won the fight via one punch knockout in the second round.

Jury faced Mike Ricci on September 21, 2013 at UFC 165.[9] Jury won the fight via split decision.

For his fifth UFC fight, Jury faced Diego Sanchez on March 15, 2014 at UFC 171.[10] He won the fight via unanimous decision.
