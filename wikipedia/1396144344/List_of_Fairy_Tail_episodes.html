Fairy Tail is an anime series based on the manga of the same name written by Hiro Mashima. Produced by A-1 Pictures and Satelight and directed by Shinji Ishihira, it was broadcast on TV Tokyo from October 12, 2009 to March 30, 2013,[1] and follows the adventures of the wizard Lucy Heartfilia after she joins the Fairy Tail wizards' guild and partners with Natsu Dragneel, who is searching for the dragon Igneel.

The series uses 28 different pieces of theme music: 14 opening themes and 14 ending themes. Several CDs containing the theme music and other tracks have been released by Pony Canyon. The first DVD compilation was released on January 29, 2010, with individual volumes being released monthly. The Southeast Asian network Animax Asia aired part of the series locally in English.[2]

In 2011, Funimation Entertainment licensed the first season for an English-language release in North America. The Funimation-dubbed episodes aired on the Funimation Channel. The first DVD set, containing 12 episodes, was released on November 22, 2011.[3] Similarly sized sets followed with four sets released as of March 20, 2012. On March 4, 2013, Mashima announced on his Twitter account that the anime will not end yet. Reruns of the anime began airing on TV Tokyo under the title Fairy Tail Best on April 4, 2013.[4] The sixth issue of the 2014 Weekly Shonen Magazine confirmed that the anime is scheduled to resume broadcasting in April 2014.[5] The thirteenth issue of 2014 "Weekly Shonen Magazine" stated that the new anime adaptation of Fairy Tail will premiere April 5, 2014 in Japan. New episodes are expected to air every Saturday at 10:30 a.m.[6]
