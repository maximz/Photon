Adam Douglas Driver (born November 19, 1983)[1][2][3] is an American actor. Since 2012, he has portrayed Adam Sackler on the HBO series Girls.

Early life[edit]

Driver was born in San Diego, California, and moved to the town of Mishawaka, Indiana, where he was raised, when he was seven years old.[4] His mother, Nancy (Needham) Wright, is a paralegal, and his father, Joe Driver, is a preacher.[3][5][6] His father's family is from Arkansas and his mother's family is from Indiana. He has English, and smaller amounts of Dutch, German, Irish, and Scottish ancestry.[7][8][9] His stepfather is a Baptist minister; Driver had a religious upbringing, and sang in choir at church.[10][11] He attended Mishawaka High School, where he graduated in 2001. He appeared in several high school plays and was an accomplished singer in the school's choir.[2]

A few months after the events of 9/11, Driver joined the United States Marine Corps. He served for two years and eight months before being deployed to Iraq.[citation needed] He injured his sternum in a biking accident, then further injured himself during a training exercise, and was medically discharged. After leaving the Marines, he attended the University of Indianapolis for a year before transferring to the Juilliard School to study drama.

Career[edit]

Following his graduation from Juilliard in 2009, Driver began acting in New York City, appearing in both Broadway and off-Broadway productions, while occasionally working as a busboy and waiter.[12] On November 21, 2013, Driver was announced to be joining the cast of Jeff Nichols' Midnight Special.[13] On January 13, 2014, Driver was added to the cast of Martin Scorsese's film, Silence. [14]

In late February 2014, Variety reported that Driver would play a villain in the 2015 film Star Wars Episode VII.[15] In March 2014, The Kennedy/Marshall Company, which is run by Lucasfilm's Kathleen Kennedy, tweeted the rumor of Driver being cast along with the hashtag #darthfader. Lena Dunham, Driver's Girls co-star, also tweeted a message, congratulating Driver on landing the role. However, when asked about it, she claimed to know nothing.[16]

Personal life[edit]

Driver married his longtime girlfriend, Joanne Tucker, on June 22, 2013.[17] He runs a non-profit organization, Arts in the Armed Forces, which stages performances for military personnel.[12] He considers John Cassavetes to be the model for the type of creative life he would like to emulate.[12]
