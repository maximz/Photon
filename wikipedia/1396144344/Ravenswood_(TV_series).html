Ravenswood was an American teen, supernatural drama, mystery-thriller television series created by I. Marlene King, Oliver Goldstick and Joseph Dougherty. A spin-off of Pretty Little Liars, ABC Family green lit the series on March 26, 2013. It premiered on October 22, 2013, following the Pretty Little Liars Halloween episode.[1] The series began filming on August 21, 2013 in New Orleans, Louisiana.[2]

On February 14, 2014, it was announced that Ravenswood was canceled after only one season.[3][4][5]

Plot[edit]

Set in the fictional town of Ravenswood, Pennsylvania, the series followed five strangers whose lives become intertwined by a deadly curse that has plagued their town for generations.[6] They have to dig into the town's dark past to solve the mysterious curse.

Cast and characters[edit]

Main cast[edit]

  • Caleb Rivers
Caleb Rivers (Tyler Blackburn), Caleb meets Miranda on the bus to Ravenswood where he's going to help his girlfriend, Hanna Marin. After Hanna meets Miranda and hears her story, she asks Caleb to stay in Ravenswood to help Miranda. She promises to visit Caleb from time to time, this could possibly mean a mixed double episode between Pretty Little Liars and Ravenswood. Caleb finds a tombstone bearing his name and picture in the town cemetery and vows to stay until he figures out what it means.
  • Miranda Collins
Miranda Collins (Nicole Gale Anderson), she meets Caleb on a bus to Ravenswood where she's going to find her uncle, Raymond Collins, her last remaining relative. Walking into the her uncle's house, she helps Hanna escape unknowing that she is Caleb's girlfriend. Miranda also finds a tombstone with her name and picture on it. Miranda died in the car accident when the car crashed into the water but her ghost is still here. Only Caleb and the others affected by the curse can see her.
  • Raymond Collins
Raymond Collins (Steven Cabral) is Miranda's uncle and runs a funeral home. He sent Miranda into foster care when her parents died over 10 years ago. Miranda comes to Ravenswood to find him.
  • Luke Matheson
Luke Matheson (Brett Dier), ia Olivia's twin brother and Remy's boyfriend. Luke doesn't trust his twin sister's friends, and vows to protect both his mother and sister as the head man in their family.
  • Olivia Matheson
Olivia Matheson (Merritt Patterson) is Luke's twin sister. Olivia is more out in the open than her brother, she cares about what people think of her mother but that doesn't stop her from living her life. Olivia formerly dated Dillion but the relationship ended after Olivia found out he killed her father.
  • Remy Beaumont
Remy Beaumont (Britne Oldford) works at the Ravenswood Gazette, the town newspaper, where her father is the editor in chief and helps Caleb find the death record of his lookalike. Remy is Luke's girlfriend and is the first to discover a theory of the curse.

Recurring cast[edit]

  • Meg Foster as Carla Grunwald. She is described as a no-nonsense, stubborn 60-year-old resident of Ravenswood. She used to be a house mother for a college. She reprises her role from Pretty Little Liars.
  • Luke Benward as Dillon Sanders, Olivia's ex-boyfriend. In the season 1 finale, he was revealed as the killer of Olivia's father and he is hit by a train at the end of the episode.
  • Henry Simmons as Simon Beaumont, Remy's father that is distrustful of out-of-towners.
  • Sophina Brown as Terry Beaumont. Terry is Remy's mother and is in the military and she just arrived home from it. She will be struggling with survivor's guilt. Like the previous survivors, Terry could be the reason for her own daughter's near death.
  • Haley Lu Richardson as Tess, Olivia's former best friend and is not involved in the curse. Tess stakes her claim on the queen bee position with Olivia off the throne.
  • Laura Allen as Rochelle Matheson, Luke and Olivia's mother. She is described as a confident woman and is now the main suspect in her husband's murder.
  • Justin Bruening as Benjamin Price, a teacher at Ravenswood High School.
  • Jay Huguley as Chief Tom Beddington.
  • Brock Kelly as Zack Springer.
  • Corrina Roshea as the Edwardian ghost, a spirit that is haunting Ravenswood and the five teens.
  • Mary Elise Hayden as Bea Grunwald.

Special guest stars[edit]

  • Ashley Benson as Hanna Marin (2 episodes)

Development and production[edit]

Casting[edit]

On April 30, 2013, it was revealed that Tyler Blackburn would reprise his Pretty Little Liars role as Caleb Rivers in Ravenswood.[7] On May 6, 2013, it was revealed that Brett Dier and Elizabeth Whitson will play twins, Abel and Olivia, in the series.[8] It was later stated that the character of Abel had been renamed Luke.[9] On May 7, 2013, it was announced that Merritt Patterson would join the show as Olivia's best friend, Tess.[10] On May 10, 2013, it was announced that Nicole Gale Anderson would join the show as Miranda.[11] In May 2013, it was announced that Meg Foster would join the cast as Mrs. Grunwald, resident of Ravenswood, making her first appearance in season 4 episode 6 of Pretty Little Liars.[11] On July 16, 2013, it was announced that Merritt Patterson would be replacing Elizabeth Whitson in circular role of Olivia.[12] Whitson has now departed Ravenswood, and Patterson has changed roles. In August 2013, it was announced that Steven Cabral joined the cast as Raymond Collins, Luke Benward joined the cast as Dillon, Henry Simmons joined the cast as Simon Beaumont, Sophina Brown joined the cast as Terry Beaumont, Haley Lu Richardson joined the cast as a replacement for Patterson's former role as Tess and Danielle Campbell joined the cast as Hayley. On August 27, 2013, it was announced that Justin Bruening joined the cast as Benjamin Price.[13] In October 2013, Jay Huguley was announced joined the cast as Chief Tom. In November 2013, Mary Elise Hayden joined the cast as Bea Hamilton.

Filming[edit]

Filming started on August 21, 2013 in New Orleans, Louisiana.[14]

Reception[edit]

Television critic David Hinckley of the New York Daily News gave the show 3 out of 5 stars saying that the show is entertaining but creepier than the parent show.[25] Scott D. Pierce of The Salt Lake Tribune said the show is ridiculous, but might be fun.[26] Margaret Lyons of New York Magazine Vulture claims the show only meets generic expectations and needs to show us more than cheap fright-gags if it wants to avoid cancellation. However, on February 14, 2014 ABC Family announced that Ravenswood would not be returning for a second season.[27]
