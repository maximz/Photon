The Big Bang Theory is an American comedy television series created and executive produced by Chuck Lorre and Bill Prady.

Like the name of the series itself, with the exception of the first episode—"Pilot"—episode titles of The Big Bang Theory always start with "The" and resemble the name of a scientific principle, theory or experiment, whimsically referencing a plot point or quirk.

The Big Bang Theory was renewed for a seventh season, which premiered on September 26, 2013.[1] The series was renewed for three additional years in March 2014, through the 2016–17 season.[2] As of March 13, 2014[update], 153 episodes have aired.

Season 8[edit]

In February 2014, CBS CEO Les Moonves confirmed an eighth season, when announcing that the first half of the new season would air on a different night, due to CBS acquiring the rights to Thursday Night Football games. The show will return to its Thursday slot that it has held for the past few seasons once the football games end.[177]

Future[edit]

In March 2014, CBS renewed the show for three additional years, through the 2016–17 season.[2]
