Golden Time („Ç¥„Éº„É´„Éá„É≥„Çø„Ç§„É†, G≈çruden Taimu?) is a Japanese light novel series written by Yuyuko Takemiya, with illustrations by ƒíji Komatsu. ASCII Media Works has published ten volumes in the series since September 2010. Plot[edit]

Banri Tada is a newly admitted student at a private law school in Tokyo. Due to the after-effects of a fall from a bridge shortly after his high school graduation, he has lost all of his memories prior to the accident (retrograde amnesia).

Banri finds himself completely lost after the opening ceremony and tries to find his way to the freshman orientation. Along the way, he runs into another lost freshman from the same school, Mitsuo Yanagisawa, and they immediately hit it off. Somehow arriving at their intended goal just in time, there appears in front of them a beautiful girl holding a bouquet of roses, who congratulates Mitsuo on getting into the school then hits him across the face with them before tossing the bouquet into his lap and leaving. This stylish, well dressed, and obsessive woman is his childhood friend, K≈çko Kaga. As children, they had promised to marry each other one day, something she has taken to heart this entire time. Mitsuo had gone out secretly and taken the examination for this private college in order to escape from her, but she used her connections to find out about it, took the law school entrance examination, and enrolled in the college herself in order to be with him.

She meets Banri Tada and a number of other students through Mitsuo, who chafes at her constant advances. Eventually he gets fed up and tells her off, crushing her hopes. Banri Tada who has become good friends with her since the term began offers her a shoulder to cry on. Eventually their mutual friendship blossoms into a romance and they become a couple. Meanwhile, Banri meets a college sophomore named Nana Hayashida, better known to everyone as "Linda". He and K≈çko join Linda's school club without realizing that Linda is, in fact, a former high school classmate of Banri's whom he had confessed to just prior to his accident. (The reason she is a sophomore and Banri is a freshman is that he essentially lost a year of his life while recovering from the accident).

Manga[edit]

A manga adaptation illustrated by Umechazuke began serialization the October 2011 issue of ASCII Media Works' Dengeki Daioh magazine. The first tank≈çbon volume was released on March 27, 2012,[12] and a total of five volumes have been released as of January 27, 2014.[13]

Anime[edit]

See also: List of Golden Time episodes

A 24-episode anime television series adaptation, produced by Genco and J.C.Staff, aired between October 3, 2013[14] and March 27, 2014 on MBS. The series is directed by Chiaki Kon with scripts by Fumihiko Shimo and character design by Shinya Hasegawa. The series' music is composed by Yukari Hashimoto.[15] For the first 12 episodes, the opening theme is "Golden Time" and the ending theme is "Sweet & Sweet Cherry". From episode 13 on, the opening theme is "The‚ô°World's‚ô°End" and the ending theme is "Han'eiky≈´teki ni Aishite yo‚ô°" (ÂçäÊ∞∏‰πÖÁöÑ„Å´ÊÑõ„Åó„Å¶„Çà‚ô°?, "Love Me Temporarily‚ô°"); all four songs are sung by Yui Horie.[16] The series is being released on eight BD/DVD compilation volumes between December 25, 2013 and July 23, 2014.[17][18] The anime has been licensed by Sentai Filmworks for streaming and home video release in 2014.[19]

Visual novel[edit]

A visual novel developed by Kadokawa Games, titled Golden Time: Vivid Memories, was released for the PlayStation Vita on March 27, 2014.[20][21]

Reception[edit]

The Mainichi Shimbun reported in March 2013 that over 710,000 copies of the light novel series have been sold in Japan.[22]
