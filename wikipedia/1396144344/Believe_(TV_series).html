Believe is an American science fiction/fantasy/adventure television series that is broadcasting as part of the 2013–14 United States network television schedule on NBC as a mid-season entry.[2] The series was created by Academy Award winner Alfonso Cuarón and Markus Friedman. The pilot episode aired March 10, 2014 before premiering in its regular time slot on Sunday, March 16.[3]

Premise[edit]

Bo is a young girl who was born with special abilities that she cannot control, and as they start evolving, the people who are protecting her must turn to an outsider. This leads them to Tate, a wrongfully convicted death-row inmate, whom they break out of prison. Although he is reluctant to take on the role as her protector, the two eventually form a bond that will guide them to helping each other as well as others while staying one step ahead of the evil forces that want the girl.

Cast[edit]

  • Johnny Sequoyah as Bo Adams, an adorable young girl who is one of Winter's most promising subjects. Bo is a special girl with extraordinary psychic and telekinetic powers, and Tate's job is to protect Bo from Winter's ex-partner; Skouras, and the army of individuals who are (and will be) attempting to kidnap her for use in their own nefarious schemes. Winter is the one person she knows she can trust. She also doesn't know that Tate is her biological father. She dislikes having to stay with Tate and how he usually suggests doing something illegal when handling a situation.
  • Jake McLaughlin as William Tate, a death row inmate in a national security prison; wrongly convicted for two counts of felony murder. He is weathered and jaded after years of confinement, but agrees to accept an elaborate prison break to free himself moments before his execution. Tate then becomes Bo's reluctant new guardian; agreeing to Winter's job to protect Bo, unaware he is her biological father. He is originally upset about being Bo's protector, but feels an emotional connection to her (though he is mostly annoyed with her).
  • Delroy Lindo as Milton Winter, the leader of a shadow organization responsible for protecting individuals with rare and incredible powers. Tate has no way of knowing if Winter's organization is good or evil, but right now he doesn't really have a choice. If he wants to maintain his freedom, he'll have to protect one of Winter's most promising subjects: a little girl named Bo. Winter and his team used to work beside Skouras and the Orchestra Project, and Winter raised Bo in the facility from infancy. At some point in the process, Winter and his entire team defected from Orchestra, took Bo, and went on the run. Winter is now enemies with Skouras.
  • Kyle MacLachlan as Roman Skouras, head of the Orchestra Project and Winter's old partner. They're both after Bo, with Skouras employing assassins and mercenaries to retrieve Bo and bring her back to Orchestra - Skouras intends to turn Bo into a weapon of frightening power for unknown reasons. Skouras is now enemies with Winter.
  • Jamie Chung as Channing, Winter's second in command. Channing is an associate of Winter's who is familiar with Bo, and is jealous of Tate's role as Bo's protector and expresses her doubts to Winter about Tate's loyalty to Bo and his ability to protect her.
  • Trieste Kelly Dunn as Agent Elizabeth Ferrell, an FBI agent in charge of the manhunt for William Tate. Skouras has solicited Ferrell's help to track down Tate and Bo. Ferrell's objective is to recapture the escaped death row inmate, but Skouras emphasizes that Bo is his top priority. Ferrell issues a nationwide Amber Alert, plastering every news channel with photos of Bo and Tate. Ferrell personally doesn't believe in paranormal abilities, but a tour of Orchestra demonstrates to her they are very real. Ferrell also wonders why Winter and a team of brilliant scientists would abandon a project like Orchestra, guessing (correctly) they broke ranks with Orchestra as they thought the goals of the organization would endanger Bo. Although Skouras assures Ferrell that this wasn't the case, she can tell he's not being entirely honest with her.

Development and production[edit]

The series first appeared as part of NBC's development slate in September 2012.[4] In January 2013, NBC green-lit production of a pilot episode.[5] On May 12, 2013, the series was placed on the network's 2013–14 schedule, where it was expected to debut after the 2014 Winter Olympics.[6]

On July 17, 2013, co-creator and executive producer Mark Friedman departed the series. Cuarón will stay on board as executive producer alongside J. J. Abrams. Friedman was ultimately succeeded by Dave Erickson.[7] A few months later, in December, Erickson also stepped down as executive producer/showrunner and was replaced by Jonas Pate, who was serving as a co-executive producer and series director. Pate now runs the day-to-day production of the series along with Hans Tobeason, who serves as "on-set" producer in New York, where filming takes place. To allow a smoother transition for the new showrunners and accommodate script re-writes, production went on hiatus from December 20 to January 6, 2014.[1]

Casting[edit]

McLaughlin (Tate) was the first regular to be cast in February 2013,[8] followed by Lindo (Winter),[9] Guillory (Moore), Chung (Channing) and 10-year-old newcomer Sequoyah (Bo).[10] MacLachlan was added in March 2013[11] as was Moayed.[12] In June 2013, Sienna Guillory dropped out of the series, though she has a large role in the pilot, along with Rami Malek. Guillory's role was not recast. Instead, Moore was taken out of the script.

Reception[edit]

Believe has received mixed reviews. On Rotten tomatoes, the show has an score of 37% based on all critics but 67% of the audience likes it.[20] On Metacritic, the critics give the show a score of 54 out of 100 while the fans give the show a 6.6 out of 10.[21]

International distribution[edit]

The series premieres in the UK on the Watch channel on March 27, 2014.[22] It airs in Canada on the CTV network,[23] and in Latin America on the Warner Channel.[24]
