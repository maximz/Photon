NCIS and its characters were introduced in a two-part episode of the CBS television series JAG in April 2003. The show premiered on September 23, 2003, in the United States.[1]

Created by Donald P. Bellisario and Don McGill, NCIS follows Supervisory Special Agent Leroy Jethro Gibbs, Senior Field Agent Anthony DiNozzo, Special Agent Timothy McGee, Special Agent Kate Todd (Seasons 1 and 2), Special Agent Ziva David (all of Seasons 3–10 and episodes 1–2 of Season 11), NSA Analyst/Probationary Special Agent Eleanor "Ellie" Bishop (Season 11, after Ziva David left), along with Forensic Scientist Abby Sciuto, Medical Examiner Dr. Donald "Ducky" Mallard, and his assistant Jimmy Palmer, all overseen by Director Leon Vance, formerly Jenny Shepard, as they investigate crimes involving Navy and Marine Corps personnel.

On March 13, 2014, CBS renewed NCIS for a twelfth season.[2] As of March 25, 2014[update], 252 original episodes of NCIS have aired.

Introductory episodes[edit]

The program and its characters are introduced during the eighth season of JAG. The JAG episodes, "Ice Queen" and "Meltdown", served as pilot episodes for the show. The character Special Agent Vivian Blackadder (Robyn Lively) does not appear in the series because producer Donald Bellisario felt that "she was a little soft for this kind of role".[35] In October 2003, the two episodes were edited together and aired as "Navy NCIS: The Beginning".[36]
