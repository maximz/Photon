The 2013–14 network television schedule for the five major English commercial broadcast networks in the United States covers primetime hours from September 2013 to August 2014. The schedule is followed by a list per network of returning series, new series, and series canceled after the 2012–13 season.

NBC was the first to announce its fall schedule on May 12, 2013,[1] followed by Fox on May 13, 2013,[2] ABC on May 14, 2013,[3] CBS on May 15, 2013[4] and The CW on May 16, 2013.[5]

PBS is not included; member stations have local flexibility over most of their schedules and broadcast times for network shows may vary. The CW is not included on weekends, when it does not offer network programming.

Cancellations/Series endings

ABC

  • The Assets—Canceled on January 10, 2014 after two low-rated episodes.[156]
  • Back in the Game—Canceled on November 1, 2013 with no more episodes set to air after the initial thirteen due to low ratings.[132]
  • Lucky 7—Canceled on October 4, 2013 after two poorly rated episodes.[157] This is the first cancellation of the season.
  • Mind Games—Canceled on March 27, 2014 after five low-rated episodes.[158]
  • Once Upon a Time in Wonderland—Canceled on March 28, 2014.[159]

CBS

  • How I Met Your Mother—It was announced on January 30, 2013 that season nine is the final season.[42]
  • We Are Men—Canceled on October 9, 2013 after two low-rated episodes.[160]

The CW

  • Nikita—It was announced on May 16, 2013 that season four is the final season. The series concluded on December 27, 2013.[161]

Fox

  • American Dad!—It was announced on July 16, 2013 that the show will move to TBS for season eleven.[162]
  • Murder Police—It was announced on October 8, 2013 that the show will not air on the network; the series has been offered to cable channels.[163]
  • Raising Hope—Canceled on March 10, 2014 after four seasons.[164]
  • Us & Them—It was announced on October 11, 2013 that production would not continue and that the seven produced episodes would air.[165]
  • The X Factor—Canceled on February 7, 2014 after three seasons, due either to low ratings or the departure of creator and judge Simon Cowell, who is returning to Britain.[166]

NBC

  • Ironside—Canceled on October 18, 2013.[167]
  • Sean Saves the World—Canceled on January 28, 2014.[168]
  • Welcome to the Family—Canceled on October 18, 2013.[167]
