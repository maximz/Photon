Alyssa Valdez is a Filipino volleyball player. She is a multi-awarded athlete and is considered to be one of the best collegiate volleyball players in the Philippines today. She is a full-time student at the Ateneo de Manila University taking up AB Psychology and a member of the student council. She is a true student-athlete as she not only excels in sports but also in academics. Being one of the most popular volleyball players in her country, she is sought after by companies as their product endorser. She is currently a member of the Ateneo de Manila University's collegiate women's varsity volleyball team in both indoor and beach volleyball.

Career Beginnings[edit]

The five-foot-nine Alyssa Valdez hails from San Juan, Batangas. She is the only girl in a brood of four siblings. She learned how to play volleyball by playing with her two older brothers when she was in elementary school at the age of 10.

Having developed her volleyball skills, she later played in the Southern Tagalog (Calabarzon) Athletic Association (STACAA), a regional sports meet for elementary and high school student athletes. After that, she was discovered and recruited by the University of Santo Tomas (UST), where she played for their high school varsity volleyball team, the UST Tigress Cubs.

High School varsity[edit]

Tigeress Cub[edit]

Alyssa Valdez studied at the University of Santo Tomas High School where she joined the UST Tigress Cubs Girls' volleyball team. Her four-year stay in UST was very productive in the field of sports. She was selected to the National Capital Region (NCR) team for the Palarong Pambansa, a national sports competition for elementary and secondary students in her first year in high school. Alyssa was UST’s top high school volleyball player who led the España-based team to several championships, three titles in UAAP, Seasons 70-72 (2007-2009), and one in Shakey's Girls' Volleyball League (Season 5). She was a two-time MVP in the UAAP (Season 71 and 72) and once in the Shakey's Girls' Volleyball League (Season 5). After playing for UST, she was recruited by Ateneo de Manila University to play for their collegiate varsity volleyball team.

College varsity[edit]

Lady Eagle[edit]

2010-2011 After graduating from UST high school in 2010, Valdez enrolled in Ateneo de Manila University for college. Though enrolled as a freshman student in Ateneo, she was not able to play for Ateneo in the school's mother league, UAAP, as she had to serve a one-year residency imposed by the University Athletic Association of the Philippines for transferring from a UAAP school to another.[1] During this time, she competed in the International Beach Volleyball Tournament in China together with Lady Eagle teammate Bea Tan.[2] She started playing for Ateneo in the Shakey's V-League.

The Shakey's V-League Season 7 2nd Conference marked Alyssa's first game appearance with the Lady Eagles in a tournament. The following Season, Ateneo bagged its first Shakey's V- League title after beating the Adamson Lady Falcons in the 1st Conference of Shakey's V-League Season 8.[3]

2011-2012 In the first ever Open Conference of Shakey's V-League, Valdez was one of the top scorers of the team. The Lady Eagles were the only collegiate team to enter the Final 4, and they finished in 3rd place.[4]

'UAAP Debut' After serving the mandatory one-year residency rule, thus making her ineligible for the UAAP Rookie of the Year award, Valdez made it to the Lady Eagles roster in the UAAP Season 74 and joined the veteran players Fille Cainglet, Dzi Gervacio, Gretchen Ho, A Nacachi, and Jem Ferrer or known collectively as the Fab 5. She contributed to the team's offense and helped bring the team to the Finals.[5] In beach volleyball, together with Fille Cainglet and Bea Tan, they finished 2nd place.[6]

In the Shakey's V-League 9th Season 1st Conference, she was named the Finals MVP after they successfully defended their title against the UST Tigresses.[7]

2012-2013 In UAAP Season 75, Valdez set a record for the most number of points scored in a game. She scored 35 points (31 spikes, 3 blocks and 1 service ace) in the second round elimination game against Adamson University in January 20, 2013. The standing record before her record breaking performance was 33 points set by an Adamson player in February 14, 2009.[8] Valdez was named the Best Scorer of UAAP Season 75. Her scoring prowess helped the Ateneo team land in the Finals of the UAAP season 75 volleyball tournament.

In the Shakey's V-League 10th Season 1st Conference, Valdez bagged the Best Server award which served as a consolation as her team unsuccessfully defended their defending championship crown against the NU Lady Bulldogs.[9]

2013-2014 Alyssa Valdez was appointed team captain of the Ateneo women's varsity volleyball team. She was named UAAP Season 76 Women's Volleyball's Best Scorer and Best Server and Season's MVP. She also bagged the Finals MVP award after beating the defending champions DLSU Women's Volleyball Team.[10]

Smart Net Spiker[edit]

Valdez played for Smart-Maynilad Net Spikers in Shakey's V-League 10th Season Open Conference where they finished 2nd place.[11][12]

National Player[edit]

She led the 8th placer RP Youth 16-Under Girl's National Volleyball Team in the 2008 Asian Youth Girls Volleyball Championship held in Pasig City, Philippines.[13]

She became a member of Philippines Women's National Volleyball Team in 2013.[14][15]

Together with the Santiago Sisters, they led the 3rd placer National team in the 2014 FIVB Asian Southeastern Zone Women's Volleyball Qualifier held in Quang Tri, Vietnam.[16][17]

Fame[edit]

Alyssa Valdez became popular nationwide when she started to play for the Ateneo Lady Eagles, the women's varsity volleyball team of the Ateneo de Manila University, undisputably the country's most popular collegiate women's volleyball team of the 2010s. She was given the moniker the Phenom for varsity volleyball by the sports press, news media and fans for her exceptional skills in volleyball. She is considered as the female counterpart of Ateneo Blue Eagle Kiefer Ravena, the "Phenom" of varsity basketball. Fans also compare her volleyball skills to the Ateneo volleyball player, former Queen Eagle, Charo Soriano. Valdez is one of the most followed volleybelles in social media, thus making her one of the sought after endorsers for sports products, gears, and drinks. She also covered on some of the famous magazines in the country and appeared on television as guest in a variety and talk shows or participant in game shows.
