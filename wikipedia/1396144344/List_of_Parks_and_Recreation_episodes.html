Parks and Recreation is an American television series created by Greg Daniels and Michael Schur. It has been billed as a companion piece to Daniels' other comedy, The Office.[1]

Produced and broadcast by NBC, Parks and Recreation is filmed in the same mockumentary style as The Office and follows Leslie Knope (Amy Poehler), a mid-level bureaucrat in the Parks and Recreation Department in Pawnee, Indiana. Inspired by local nurse Ann Perkins (Rashida Jones), Leslie vows to turn a vacant lot with a large pit into a beautiful park. Also starring are Aziz Ansari as Tom Haverford, Leslie's co-worker; Nick Offerman as Ron Swanson, the head of the Parks and Recreation department and Leslie's superior; Aubrey Plaza as April Ludgate, Ron's assistant, Jim O'Heir as Jerry Gergich and Retta as Donna Meagle, other employees of the Parks and Recreation department; Chris Pratt as Andy Dwyer, married to April and works as a shoe-shiner at Pawnee City Hall; and Adam Scott as Ben Wyatt and Rob Lowe as Chris Traeger, state auditors tasked with evaluating the Parks and Recreation department, introduced in the penultimate episode of the show's second season.

In March 2014, NBC renewed the show for a seventh season. As of March 20, 2014[update], 107 episodes of Parks and Recreation have aired.

Season 7[edit]

On January 19, 2014, NBC entertainment chairman Bob Greenblatt said that the show "is going to have a seventh season,"[120][121] adding "We’ll have to look at how much comedy will be on the schedule in the fall and where it will land but I think both [Parks and Recreation and Community] are strong possibilities for returning."[122] The renewal was confirmed on March 19, 2014.[123]
