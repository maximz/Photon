The 2014 NCAA Women's Division I Basketball Tournament will be played in March and April 2014, with the Final Four played April 6–8.[1] The subregional and regional locations are below. The Final Four will be played at the Bridgestone Arena[2][3] in Nashville, Tennessee.[4] The Ohio Valley Conference will serve as the host institution.[5] This is the first time that Nashville has hosted a Women's Final Four Basketball tournament.[6] Tennessee continued its streak of making every NCAA Women's Basketball Tournament at 33 consecutive appearances.

Subregionals[edit]

The format is similar to the Men's Tournament, except that there are 64 teams; this in turn means there is no "First Four" round. Thirty-one automatic bids for conference tournament champions plus one automatic bid for the Ivy League regular-season champion, and 32 at-large bids are available.

The subregionals will be played from March 22 through March 25, 2014.[7] Sites chosen to host first- and second-round games in 2014 include:

The Regionals, named for the city rather than the region of geographic importance since 2005, which will be held from March 29 to April 1, will be at these sites:[8][9]

  • March 29 and 31
    • Lincoln Regional, Pinnacle Bank Arena, Lincoln, Nebraska (Host: Nebraska)
    • Notre Dame Regional, Joyce Center, Notre Dame, Indiana (Host: Notre Dame)
  • March 30 and April 1
    • Louisville Regional, KFC Yum! Center, Louisville, Kentucky (Host: Louisville)
    • Stanford Regional, Maples Pavilion, Stanford, California (Host: Stanford)

The Final Four will be held on April 6 & 8 at the Bridgestone Arena[2][3] in Nashville, Tennessee.[4]

Media coverage[edit]

Television[edit]

ESPN has US television rights to all games during the tournament through 2024.[11] For the first and second round, ESPN airs select games nationally on ESPN, ESPNU, or ESPNews. All other games are aired regionally on ESPN or ESPN2 and streamed online via ESPN3. Most of the nation gets whip-a-round coverage during this time, which allows ESPN to rotate between the games and focus on the nation on the one that is the closest. The regional semifinals are split between ESPN and ESPN2, and ESPN airs the regional finals, national semifinals, and championship match.[12]
