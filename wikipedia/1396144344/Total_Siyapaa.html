Total Siyapaa (formerly Aman Ki Asha) is a Hindi Indian romantic comedy film directed by E. Nivas and written by Neeraj Pandey.[3] It released on 7 March 2014 distributed by Reliance Big Entertainment across 850 theatres in India with two other films: Gulaab Gang and Queen.[4] The film opened on a similar note with Queen in multiplexes offering stiff competition to each other owing to a similar release count.[4]

The film stars Ali Zafar and Yami Gautam in lead roles[5] with Anupam Kher and Kiron Kher in supporting roles—who are appearing for the first time as an on-screen couple.[6] The first look of the film along with that of Holiday: A Soldier Is Never Off Duty was launched on the Zee Cine Awards.[7][8]

Gada-owned Pen India acquired the rights of the film from Reliance Entertainment while & Pictures acquired 50 per cent equity of the satellite rights. Reliance and Zee Network own the film's overseas and promotional rights respectively. Consequently, Reliance and Gada decided to postpone the release date to 7 March 2014 from 30 January 2014 to promote the film.[9]

Critical reception

Total Siyapaa received mostly negative reviews, with critics suggesting skipping this movie.[10] Reviews of the performances were mixed, with the Hindustan Times lauding it[11] while the Deccan Chronicle and Bollywood Life criticizing Ali Zafar's and Yami Gautam's performances, respectively.[12][13] Kirron Kher's performance was seen as one of the strong points of the movie.[12][13] The lack of a solid plot,[11][14] the lack of attention to detail[15] and poor comic timing were seen as major drawbacks.[16] The film was largely seen as unfunny[11][12][13][17] and not living up to the trailer.[11][14][18]

Box office reception

Total Siyapaa received a low opening[19][20] with poor Box Office reception in India.[21][22][23] due to negative reviews from critics[22] However, the film did better overseas,[24] being positively received in Pakistan and the United Arab Emirates.[25]

The album is sung and composed by Ali Zafar who is also the main lead of the movie. Music was released on 14 February 2014 and was well received by Critics. Karthik writes "The title song Total Siyaapa along with the quirky lyrics, dialogues and sounds, is good fun. Ali Zafar fares really well in Palat Meri jaan, singing, writing and composing the lovely ballad. Palat Meri Jaan is a Chart buster. Nahi maloom has a captivating Middle-Eastern + Latino blend sound and is sung particularly well by Ali and Fariha Parvaiz. The composer-singer uses a reggae template in Asha and delivers a nice, winsome song. Chal buleya is sufi song with a message. Barring that minor misstep, charming soundtrack overall, with Ali offering a good demonstration of his multi-facetedness.[27][28][29]
